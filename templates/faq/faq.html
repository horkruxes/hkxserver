What is the goal of this website?

The goal is to provide a micro-blogging space like Twitter but decentralized and where users have some sovereignty over their power to speak, unlike big social network where you can get banned or censored. Also, it allows anyone to speak anonymously without revealing themselves. Here, jounalists and whistleblowers can speak freely.

How do I post a message?

This website doesn't have any authentication. You have to handle yourself the authentication by creating keys that only YOU will manage.

First step: create a key pair

Second step: write a message and sign it

Third step: send the message and the signature

What is a pod?

Here, you are visiting a pod, but there are many more existing. You have to choose a good pod, managed by someone you trust. You can post to any pod you want, at anytime. TODO A pod can be secret, known, public, private...

What does "being registered on a pod" means?

Anyone can post on a pod, but when the public key isn't known from the pod, there are limitations (only a message/pod each minute to prevent spam, but you can choose to post on another pod if you are in a hurry). Being registered on a pod is a contract between you and the pod admin (not between you and a big company that seeks only profit!). The pod admin will "whitelist" your public key. You can post whenever you want, and the pod admin trust that you will not flood the pod.

This site is absolutely not practical. Why did you make these choices?

The way the website is structured, I (the creator) will have absolutely NO CONTROL on your data. This might be not practical, but it is secured. Moreover, this way only serious people will post things, no spam nor useless messages.

What does "wrong signature" means?

If there is the mark "wrong signature", it means that a hacker on the pod admin have modified the database. Don't trust this message. You can hide them if you want.

What can happen if someone hack the website?

Your password isn't stored in the server because... You know... There isn't a password. You'll loose nothing. The only thing that can happen is your messages to be altered (but thanks to the signature, other users will be warned that it has been modified) or deleted. If you want to prevent this to happen, it's better to use a pod that is administrated by someone you know and trust.

I lost my secret key, how can I get it back?

You can't. You must use another key, and try to explain to your followers that the new public key it you.
